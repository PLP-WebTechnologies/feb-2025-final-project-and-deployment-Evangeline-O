Ugali Recipe

Kenya's Staple Food

Prep: 5 mins | Cook: 25 mins Servings: 4

Ugali is the staple food of Kenya, a thick porridge made from maize flour that accompanies almost every meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups maize flour (preferably Kenyan maize flour)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (optional)

Instructions

  1. Bring the water to a boil in a heavy-bottomed saucepan.
  2. Reduce heat to medium and gradually add the maize flour while stirring continuously with a wooden spoon.
  3. Keep stirring as the mixture thickens to prevent lumps from forming.
  4. As it thickens, continue stirring and turning the ugali, pressing it against the sides of the pot to help it come together.
  5. After about 15-20 minutes of cooking and stirring, the ugali should pull away from the sides of the pot and form a firm mass.
  6. Turn it out onto a serving plate and shape it into a round mound.
  7. Serve immediately while hot with your favorite stew or sukuma wiki.

Chef's Tips

  • For authentic Kenyan ugali, use specially ground ugali flour available in African grocery stores.
  • The key to perfect ugali is constant stirring to avoid lumps.
  • Traditionally, ugali is eaten by pulling off a small piece with your fingers and using it to scoop up stew or vegetables.
